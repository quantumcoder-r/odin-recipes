Dhokla Recipe

Steps To Make Dhokla
  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the besan, suji, turmeric powder, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the oil, ginger paste, chili paste, and half of the water. Whisk until completely smooth and free of lumps.
  3. Add the lemon juice and stir well. The batter should have the consistency of a thick, pourable cake batter.
  4. Grease a deep round or square cake tin (or flat dhokla plates) lightly with oil.
  5. Fill a steamer or a large, wide pot with about 2 inches of water and bring it to a rolling boil over medium heat.
  6. Right before pouring the batter into the greased pan, add the Eno fruit salt into the batter and quickly pour 1 tsp of water over it
  7. Whisk gently in one direction for 30–60 seconds until the batter becomes frothy, light, and doubles in volume.
  8. Immediately pour the frothy batter into the greased tin and place it on a steamer stand inside your pot.
  9. Insert a knife or toothpick in the center; if it comes out clean, it is done. Let it cool slightly before removing
  10. Top with finely chopped coriander leaves and grated fresh coconut. Serve with green mint-coriander chutney.
