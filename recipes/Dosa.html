Dosa Recipe

Steps To Make Dosa
  1. Make the Batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine the semolina, rice flour, and maida. Whisk in the curd, water, salt, cumin seeds, and chopped green chili until smooth. The batter should be watery and thin, similar to a crepe consistency
  2. Rest: Let the batter rest for 15-20 minutes so the semolina can absorb the liquid.
  3. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa (skillet) over medium-high heat. Lightly grease it with a few drops of oil.
  4. Pour and Spread: Stir the batter thoroughly (as it tends to settle at the bottom). Pour a ladleful of batter from the outer edges of the pan towards the center. Do not spread it with the ladle; just let it naturally flow to create a lace-like, thin dosa.
  5. Roast: Drizzle a teaspoon of oil or ghee around the edges and on top. Cook on medium-low heat until the bottom turns golden brown and the edges start lifting.
  6. Serve: Fold the dosa and serve immediately with fresh coconut chutney.
