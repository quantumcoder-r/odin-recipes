Dhokla Recipe

Steps To Make Dhokla
  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, carom seeds, and salt.
  2. Rub the ghee or oil into the flour with your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs and clumps together.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a firm, tight dough (do not make it too soft). Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Heat \(1\) tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  5. Add the mashed potatoes, garam masala, chaat masala, and salt. Mix well while lightly mashing the potatoes to create a flavorful, chunky texture.
  6. Divide your rested dough into equal, golf-ball-sized portions.Roll one ball into an oval shape (about 6-7 inches long). Cut the oval exactly down the middle to create two semi-circles.
  7. Take one semi-circle, apply a little water to the straight edge, and fold it over to form a cone. Press the seam together to seal it tightly.
  8. Heat a deep pan of oil over medium-low heat. Samosas must be fried slowly to keep the crust crispy and not blistered.Carefully drop 3 to 4 samosas into the oil. Fry them slowly for 10-12 minutes, turning them occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy.
  9. Remove them with a slotted spoon and drain on a paper towel. Serve hot with green chutney or sweet tamarind chutney!
